## 1. CLIMATE DATA — Rainfall Trends

### Current Amsterdam Rainfall
- **Annual precipitation**: ~850 mm/year (Amsterdam, 1991–2020 avg)
  Source: KNMI climate normals; climate-data.org; climatestotravel.com
- **Monthly range**: 39.6 mm (April, driest) to 98.6 mm (August, wettest)
  Source: climatestotravel.com — Amsterdam climate data
- **95th percentile hourly intensity (Amsterdam)**: 6.1 mm/hr
- **99th percentile hourly intensity (Amsterdam summer)**: 8.5 mm/hr (July–August)
  Source: Manola et al. (2020). "Analysis of urban rainfall from hourly to seasonal scales using high-resolution radar observations in the Netherlands." International Journal of Climatology. https://doi.org/10.1002/joc.6241

### Historical Increase
- **Annual precipitation increase**: +25% over the past century in NL
- **Winter increase**: +35%; **Summer increase**: +16%
  Source: Buishand et al. (2013), cited in Manola et al. (2020)
- **Extreme hourly intensity already 10–15% higher** than before 1990 (attributable to ~1°C warming)
  Source: KNMI Local Precipitation project — https://www.knmiprojects.nl/projects/future-weather/local-precipitation

### Scaling Relationship (Key Fact)
- **Extreme hourly rainfall increases 14% per degree of warming** (2x Clausius-Clapeyron)
- **A doubling of intensity occurs with each 5.3°C temperature rise**
  Source: Lenderink & van Meijgaard (2008). "Increase in hourly precipitation extremes beyond expectations from temperature changes." Nature Geoscience 1, 511–514. https://doi.org/10.1038/ngeo262
  Also: KNMI Projects — https://www.knmiprojects.nl/projects/future-weather/local-precipitation

### Future Projections (KNMI'23 Scenarios)
- **Temperature rise by 2050**: +1 to +2.3°C (above 2014 baseline)
  Source: Van den Hurk et al. (2014), cited in Manola et al. (2020); IEA Netherlands Climate Resilience Indicator
- **Annual mean precipitation by 2050**: +2.5% to +5.5% (above 1981–2010)
- **Winter precipitation**: +3% to +17%
- **Summer precipitation**: up to −13%
  Source: IEA (2022). "Netherlands Climate Resilience Policy Indicator." https://www.iea.org/articles/netherlands-climate-resilience-policy-indicator
- **KNMI'23 four scenarios**: Hd, Hn, Ld, Ln — all project wetter winters, more severe downpours, drier summers
  Source: Van de Wiel et al. (2024). "KNMI'23 Climate Scenarios for the Netherlands." Earth's Future. https://doi.org/10.1029/2023EF003983

### Derived Projection (using the 14%/degree scaling)
- 1°C warming → 14% increase in extreme hourly rainfall
- 2°C warming → ~30% increase
- 2.3°C warming → ~37% increase
  These are calculations applied to the KNMI 14%/degree finding.

## 2. DRAINAGE SYSTEM DATA

### Amsterdam Sewer Capacity
- **Design capacity**: 20 mm/hour (standard Dutch norm, once-per-2-year event)
  Source: Brolsma et al. (2016). "The Rainproof City." Published via openresearch.amsterdam. https://openresearch.amsterdam/en/page/68041/the-rainproof-city
- **Amsterdam 2050 target**: Handle 60 mm/hour without damage
  Source: Amsterdam Rainproof / Weerproof knelpuntenkaart: https://maps.amsterdam.nl/rainproof/
- **Sewer overflows approximately 4 times per year** during heavy rainfall
  Source: Waternet — https://www.waternet.nl/en/our-water/sewer-water/combined-sewer-system/
- **Amsterdam has peat soil**, making sewer expansion difficult → strategy focuses on storage
  Source: Dai et al. (2018). "Rainproof cities in the Netherlands." International Journal of Water Resources Development 34(4), 652–674. https://doi.org/10.1080/07900627.2017.1372273

### 3Di Model Simulations (used by Amsterdam)
- The Amsterdam Rainproof knelpuntenkaart is based on **3Di hydrodynamic model**
- Simulates a **60 mm/hr storm lasting 2 hours** (total 120 mm)
- Uses: elevation data (AHN2), resistance map, infiltration map, sewer model
- The Waterdieptekaart simulates **120 mm in 2 hours** showing flood depth
  Source: maps.amsterdam.nl/rainproof — legend and methodology text

## 3. PLANTAGEBUURT SPECIFIC DATA

### Flood Vulnerability
- **Plantagebuurt is one of the most urgent rainwater bottleneck areas** in Amsterdam
  Source: Erfgoeddeal project description — https://www.erfgoeddeal.nl/projecten/water-en-bodem/artis-ruimte-voor-water-in-de-stad
  Also: ARTIS official news — https://www.artis.nl/nl/artis-park/nieuws-uit-artis-park/plantagebuurt-en-artis-vangen-regenwater-op
- **The neighborhood is low-lying**, making the sewer less able to drain water
  Source: ARTIS news (above); Buurtorganisatie 1018 — https://www.buurtorganisatie1018.nl/
- **Water storage need**: Arcadis report "Amsterdam Klimaatbestendig 2050" estimates
  the Plantagebuurt needs storage of **>200 m³ per hectare** (based on 70 mm/hr in 1 hour simulation)
  Source: Buurtorganisatie 1018 questionnaire article (Dec 2025) — https://www.buurtorganisatie1018.nl/nieuws-uit-1018/2025/12/22/vragenlijst-wateroverlast-in-de-plantagebuurt
- **Resident experiences**: basements flooding, residents installing pumps, waterproofing spaces
  Source: Buurtorganisatie 1018 (above)

### Knelpuntenkaart Priority
- Amsterdam categorizes rainwater bottlenecks as:
  - **Red (extremely urgent)**: risk of serious property damage, vital infrastructure, hospitals, disrupted access
  - **Orange (very urgent)**: risk of property damage and traffic disruption
  - **Yellow (urgent)**: risk of property damage
- **Goal**: Red solved within 5 years, Orange within 10, Yellow within 15
  Source: Weerproof — https://weerproof.nl/regenwaterknelpuntenkaart/

## 4. ARTIS INTERVENTION DATA (Erfgoeddeal Project)

### Project: "ARTIS: Ruimte voor Water in de Stad"
- **Funding**: €500,000 from Erfgoeddeal (8th round, Sep 2024)
- **Partners**: Municipality of Amsterdam, Waternet, Waterschap Amstel Gooi en Vecht, AMS Institute, TU Delft
- **Key intervention**: Large water buffer under the redesigned **Salmplein** (to become a new public square)
- **Connection**: Plantage Middenlaan linked to Salmplein, allowing ARTIS to receive excess neighborhood rainwater
- **Historical restoration**: The **Watertuin** (Water Garden), originally designed by landscape architect **Leonard Springer**, will be restored to function as a modern buffering system
- **Historic water pond** in front of the Aquarium will be fed with captured neighborhood rainwater
- **ARTIS water reuse**: Captured rainwater replaces tap water for gardens and animal enclosures
- **Timeline**: First works at ARTIS Aquarium started 2025; full completion expected ~2030
- **Public engagement**: Salmplein will be made publicly accessible; includes water play elements, shade, greenery
  Source: Erfgoeddeal — https://www.erfgoeddeal.nl/projecten/water-en-bodem/artis-ruimte-voor-water-in-de-stad
  Source: ARTIS official — https://www.artis.nl/nl/artis-park/nieuws-uit-artis-park/plantagebuurt-en-artis-vangen-regenwater-op
  Source: Land+Water — https://landenwater.nl/nieuws/artis-en-plantagebuurt-vangen-regenwater-op
  Source: Buurtorganisatie 1018 — https://www.buurtorganisatie1018.nl/nieuws-uit-1018/2024/10/21/

### ARTIS Existing Water Practices
- ARTIS already has **underground rainwater storage tanks** distributed across the park
- Uses **canal water** for animal enclosures (Savannah, elephants)
- **Toilets in ARTIS-Groote Museum** flushed with harvested rainwater (not tap water)
- Future plan: all toilets to use canal water or rainwater
- Goal: **operate without ecological footprint by 2030**
  Source: artis.nl (2024); Kort et al. (2023). "Outcomes from the First European Planetary Health Congress at ARTIS." Challenges 14(4), 49. https://doi.org/10.3390/challe14040049

## 5. AMSTERDAM RAINPROOF APPROACH

### Three-Step Strategy (Dutch national water management)
1. **Capture** rainwater where it falls
2. **Store** it locally (wadis, green infrastructure, underground tanks)
3. **Drain** only when necessary
  Source: Dai et al. (2018). "Rainproof cities in the Netherlands." https://doi.org/10.1080/07900627.2017.1372273

### Amsterdam Rainproof Platform
- Network aiming to make Amsterdam rainproof by 2050
- Started 2014 after a major cloudburst caused €10M in damage (July 28, 2014 — return period 5–15 years)
- Coalition of citizens, government, businesses, knowledge institutions
  Source: Amsterdam Rainproof / Amsterdam Smart City — https://amsterdamsmartcity.com/updates/project/amsterdam-rainproof
  Source: Interlace Hub — https://interlace-hub.com/rainproof-amsterdam
  Source: IWA — https://www.iwa-network.org/our-work/amsterdam
